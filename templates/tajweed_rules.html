Tajweed Rules

أَحْكَام التَّجْوِيد

Tajweed is the set of rules governing the proper pronunciation of the Quran. These rules help ensure that the Quran is recited as closely as possible to how it was revealed to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. The Qur'an flows like water — smooth and full of life. Tajweed is the subtle bed beneath, guiding each sound with grace and purpose.

خَيْرُكُمْ مَنْ تَعَلَّمَ الْقُرْآنَ وَعَلَّمَهُ
"The best among you are those who learn the Quran and teach it to others."
Sahih al-Bukhari 5027

Madd (Prolongation)

When certain letters are followed by specific signs, their sound should be extended. This rule applies to the letters ا, و, and ي when they have a sukoon or when followed by a hamza.

فِيهَا ، قَالُوا ، آمَنُوا

Ghunnah (Nasal Sound)

A nasal sound that occurs with the letters ن and م when they have a shaddah. The sound should be held for approximately two counts.

ثُمَّ ، إِنَّ ، مِمَّا

Qalqalah (Echoing Sound)

An echoing sound that occurs with five letters (ق ط ب ج د) when they have a sukoon or at the end of a word. The sound bounces back with a slight echo.

يَجْعَلْ ، أَحَد ، خَلَقْ

Ikhfa (Concealment)

When a noon sakinah or tanween is followed by any of the 15 ikhfa letters, the noon sound is hidden and pronounced with a nasal sound.

مِنْ قَبْلُ ، عَذَابٌ شَدِيدٌ

Ikhfa Meem Saakin

When a meem with sukoon is followed by the letter ب, it is pronounced with a light nasal sound while hiding the meem.

هُم بِمُؤْمِنِين

Idghaam (Merging)

When noon sakinah or tanween is followed by specific letters (ي ر م ل و ن), the noon sound merges into the following letter.

مِن نَّذِيرٍ ، خَيْرٌ لَّكُمْ

Silent Letters

Certain letters in the Quran are written but not pronounced during recitation. These letters are usually marked with a small circle above them.

اهْدِنَا ، قَالُوا

Common Mistakes to Avoid

⚠️

Madd Duration

Not extending the madd letters for their proper duration. Remember: 2, 4, or 6 counts based on the type.

⚠️

Ghunnah Sound

Skipping the nasal sound in noon and meem mushaddad. Always hold these for 2 counts.

⚠️

Qalqalah Clarity

Not pronouncing the qalqalah letters with their characteristic bounce, especially at stops.
