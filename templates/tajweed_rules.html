Tajweed Rules

أَحْكَام التَّجْوِيد

Tajweed is the set of rules governing the proper pronunciation of the Quran. These rules help ensure that the Quran is recited as closely as possible to how it was revealed to Prophet Muhammad ﷺ. The Qur'an flows like water — smooth and full of life. Tajweed is the subtle bed beneath, guiding each sound with grace and purpose.

خَيْرُكُمْ مَنْ تَعَلَّمَ الْقُرْآنَ وَعَلَّمَهُ
"The best among you are those who learn the Quran and teach it to others."
Sahih al-Bukhari 5027

Ghunnah

الغُنَّة

Ghunnah is the nasalization that occurs with the letters ن and م. It must be held for 2 counts when these letters have a shaddah (ّ).

إِنَّ ، ثُمَّ

Idghaam

الإدغام

When a noon sakinah (نْ) or tanween meets certain letters, it merges into the following letter. The letters of Idghaam are ي ر م ل و ن.

مِنْ رَّبِّهِمْ

Ikhfa

الإخفاء

When a noon sakinah (نْ) or tanween is followed by any of the 15 Ikhfa letters, the noon sound is hidden and pronounced with a nasal sound.

مِنْ قَبْلِ

Qalqalah

القَلْقَلَة

The letters ق ط ب ج د when having a sukoon produce an echo-like sound. This is more pronounced when stopping on these letters.

قُلْ أَعُوذُ

Iqlab

الإقْلاب

When noon sakinah (نْ) or tanween is followed by the letter ب, the noon sound is converted to a meem sound with ghunnah.

مِنْ بَعْدِ

Idghaam Shafawi

الإدغام الشَّفَوي

When a meem sakinah (مْ) meets another meem, it merges into it with a ghunnah sound held for 2 counts.
